# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Be respectful** - Treat everyone with respect and consideration
- **Be constructive** - Provide helpful, actionable feedback
- **Be collaborative** - Work together to solve problems
- **Be patient** - Remember that everyone has different experience levels
- **Be inclusive** - Welcome newcomers and help them get started
- **Be gracious** - Accept constructive criticism gracefully

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, trolling, or personal attacks
- Discriminatory language or jokes
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Deliberate intimidation or threatening behavior
- Sustained disruption of discussions

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories (issues, PRs, discussions)
- Community forums and chat channels
- In-person or virtual events
- Direct communications regarding project work

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

1. Opening a private issue with project maintainers
2. Contacting a maintainer directly via email

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.

### Process

1. **Report received** - Maintainers review the report
2. **Investigation** - Gather information from all parties
3. **Decision** - Determine appropriate action
4. **Action** - Implement the decision
5. **Follow-up** - Ensure the situation is resolved

### Consequences

Depending on the severity and nature of the violation:

| Level | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 1. Warning | Private warning with explanation |
| 2. Temporary Ban | Temporary exclusion from community spaces |
| 3. Permanent Ban | Permanent exclusion from community spaces |
